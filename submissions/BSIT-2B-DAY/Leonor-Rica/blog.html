Haunted Boarding House I Will Never Forget

Written by: Rica A. Leonor

September 20, 2025

When I moved to the city for college, I was just like any other student—eager, broke, and desperate for a cheap place to stay. I thought I struck gold when I found an old boarding house just a few streets away from campus. The rent was low, the landlady seemed welcoming, and the location was perfect.

“But sometimes, cheap comes with a price you never expect.”

The first few nights were fine. My room was small but decent: a bed, a desk, and a creaky old wooden cabinet. The house itself felt a little too quiet, but I brushed it off. However, things started to change after the first week. Then came the fourth night. I was woken up by the sound of footsteps outside my door. When I peeked through the peephole, no one was there. The footsteps continued, pacing back and forth, growing louder and more deliberate.

I tried to convince myself it was just my imagination, but the next night, the footsteps returned. This time, they were accompanied by a faint whispering sound. Instead of knocking, the wooden cabinet inside my room slowly creaked open. Two pale hands stretched out from the darkness, followed by a face with sunken eyes, peeling skin, and a grin too wide to belong to any living thing.

It crawled forward, whispering in a rasp that froze my blood: “You took my room.” I tried to run, but the door slammed shut by itself. The figure inched closer—until it slipped back inside the cabinet, leaving me trembling in the dark.

The next morning, I packed my bags and left the boarding house without looking back. I never found out who—or what—haunted that place, but I learned a valuable lesson:

“Sometimes, cheap comes with a price you never expect — the cost of your sanity or your soul.”

So I’ll leave you with this question: Would you dare stay in that boarding house?
